# What is HCIS?
HCIS (High Commission for Industrial Security) oversees the protection of strategic industries and critical infrastructure in Saudi Arabia. It issues **Security Directives** (e.g., SEC-02 Security Fencing Systems, SEC-03 Security Gates, SEC-05 Physical Security) that define minimum requirements for perimeter fencing, access control, intrusion detection, and related systems.

Compliance with HCIS is **mandatory** for facilities under its scope. Non-compliance can prevent project approval or operation.

# HCIS Fencing Classification (Classes 1–4)
HCIS classifies perimeters based on the security risk level of the facility. Higher classes demand stronger delay times against intrusion tools and vehicles.

### Typical HCIS Fence Classes (Industry Standards Referenced for Hitech Solutions)
- **Class 1 (Highest Security – Critical Facilities)**:  
  Comprehensive multi-layered defense. Includes external and internal clear zones (e.g., 4.6m patrol roads + 6m clear zones). Requires anti-personnel and anti-vehicle barriers, high-delay fencing, multiple layers of concertina razor tape, and integration with CCTV/intrusion detection. Often demands crash-rated elements.

- **Class 2 (High Security)**:  
  Strong physical barriers with anti-climb/anti-cut features, typically 3m+ height rigid welded mesh or chain link with concertina topping.

- **Class 3 & 4 (Medium to Lower Security)**:  
  Standard high-security mesh or chain link fences (minimum 3m height in many cases), with one or two layers of razor tape. Focus on deterrence and delay rather than maximum resistance.

Key technical features across HCIS-compliant fences often include:
- Minimum height: 2.5m–3m+
- Rigid welded mesh or chain link construction
- Anti-climb / anti-cut design (small mesh openings)
- Hot-dip galvanization + optional PVC coating for corrosion resistance in Saudi climate
- Topping with concertina/razor wire (1–3 layers depending on class)
- Delay rating against aggressive tools (reference to ASTM F2781-15 in many implementations)
- Integration readiness with gates, lighting, and electronic security systems

# HCIS Requirements for Perimeter Fencing (Key Elements)
- **Design & Layout**: Clear zones inside/outside the fence, patrol roads, and no blind spots.
- **Materials & Construction**: High-tensile steel, secure welding, uniform coating.
- **Performance**: Must delay intrusion long enough for response forces. Anti-vehicle and anti-personnel capabilities vary by class.
- **Installation & Testing**: Professional installation with proper documentation. Systems must pass HCIS verification.
- **Certification**: Products and installers should demonstrate compliance through approved vendors or consultants.
- **Integration**: Fencing works as part of a holistic system including gates (SEC-03), lighting, and intrusion detection.
