# Summary (Primary retrieval anchor)
Anti-Vehicle Barriers (AVB), also known as Hostile Vehicle Mitigation (HVM) systems, form a critical layer in HCIS Category 1 perimeter security. They are crash-rated physical barriers designed to stop or significantly delay vehicle-borne threats (including heavy trucks used as weapons). Under HCIS SEC-02 and SEC-06, Category 1 requires a certified AVB in addition to the anti-personnel fence. Hitech Fence & Steel supports HCIS-compliant projects by providing robust fencing systems that integrate with approved AVB solutions, ensuring full layered defense for critical Saudi facilities.

# Role of Anti-Vehicle Barriers in HCIS Category 1
In HCIS Category 1 (highest security level), the perimeter system includes a dedicated **anti-vehicle layer**. This layer works alongside:
- External and internal clear zones
- Anti-personnel fence (3m+ rigid mesh with triple concertina wire)
- Intrusion detection, lighting, and surveillance

The AVB prevents unauthorized vehicles from ramming through the perimeter, protecting against vehicle-borne improvised explosive devices (VBIED) or forced entry.

# HCIS Requirements for Anti-Vehicle Barriers
- **Mandatory for Category 1**: An anti-vehicle barrier must be installed as part of the layered defense.
- **Referenced Directive**: Follows HCIS SEC-06 (Anti-Vehicle Barriers) in conjunction with SEC-02 (Security Fencing Systems).
- **Crash Rating Standard**: Must comply with **ASTM F2656** (or equivalent international crash test standard).
  - Common rating for Category 1: **M50 P1** (stops a 6,800 kg / 6.8-ton vehicle traveling at 80 km/h / 50 mph with minimal penetration — typically ≤1 meter).
- **Performance Criteria**:
  - Zero or very low penetration distance after impact.
  - Ability to disable the attacking vehicle.
  - Maintain structural integrity to prevent breaching.
- **Installation Location**: Usually placed parallel to or just in front of the anti-personnel fence, within the external or internal interface area.
- **Clear Zones**: Must integrate with the required 6m external and 10m internal clear zones for visibility and response.

# Types of Anti-Vehicle Barriers Commonly Used in HCIS Projects
- **Road Blockers / Bollards**: Rising bollards or fixed crash-rated bollards.
- **Crash-Rated Fences / Rope Barriers**: Specialized high-strength rope or cable barriers designed for vehicle impact.
- **Wedge Barriers / Plate Barriers**: Hydraulic or manual rising wedge systems.
- **Anti-Ram Gates**: Reinforced gates with AVB protection.
- **Concrete or Steel Barriers**: Fixed or movable heavy barriers (less common for permanent perimeters).

**Example Performance (Industry Standard for HCIS Category 1)**:
- Stops 6.8-ton vehicle at 50 mph (80 km/h) with ≤1m penetration (ASTM F2656 M50 P1).
- Provides both physical stop and visual deterrence.
