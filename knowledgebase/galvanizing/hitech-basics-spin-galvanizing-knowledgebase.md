# Spin Galvanizing Process

## What is Spin Galvanizing?
Spin galvanizing (also known as centrifugal galvanizing) is a finishing process in which small metal objects such as nuts and bolts are coated with zinc by means of high-speed spinning. This process provides corrosion protection, accuracy, and durability through the use of modern spin technology.

## End-to-End Automated Process

### Step 1: Pretreatment
- **Degreasing** - Remove oils and contaminants
- **Pickling** - Acid cleaning to remove rust/scale
- **Fluxing** - Prepare surface for zinc adhesion
- Process-controlled for consistency

### Step 2: Robotic Hot-Dip
- **Consistent immersion** - Automated dipping
- **Controlled dwell time** - Precise coating thickness
- **Temperature monitoring** - Optimal zinc bath temperature
- Quality assured at every stage

### Step 3: Centrifugal Spin-Off
- **High RPM spinning** - Removes excess zinc
- **Radial movement** - Parts move outward
- **Center remains clear** - No zinc buildup
- **Thread-safe finish** - Clean, usable threads

### Step 4: Post-Treatment & QA
- **Passivation** - Chemical treatment for enhanced protection
- **Cooling** - Controlled cooling process
- **Thickness testing** - Verify coating thickness
- **Adhesion checks** - Ensure quality bonding
- **Batch documentation** - Full traceability
